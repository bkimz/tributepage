Emmanuel "Manny" Dapidran Pacquiao

The man who became a boxing legend from nothing. The world's only eight division world champion. The first ever fighting senator.

Manny Pacquiao (right) knocks out Ricky Hatton in the 2nd round to win the IBO and The Ring light welterweight champion title.

Here are some key events in Emmanuel "Manny Dapidran Pacquiao's life:

  • 1978 - born in Kibawe, Bukidnon, Philippines.
  • 1993 - at the age of 14 Pacquiao moves to Manila to start his boxing career. He goes on to have an amateur record of 60-4.
  • 1995 - the death of his close friend, Eugene Barutag spurs Pacquiao to pursue professional boxing.
  • 1998 - Defeats Chatchai Sasakul to take the WBC flyweight title. Pacquiao's first world title.
  • 2001 - Pacquiao moves up to super bantamweight and defeats Lehlohonolo Ledwaba for the IBF Super Bantamweight title. Pacquaio's 2nd world title.
  • 2003 - Pacquiao fights Marco Antonio Barrera in the featherweight division winning by TKO on the eleventh round for The Ring and lineal featherweight championship. Pacquiao becomes the first Asian to become a three-division world champion.
  • 2005 - Pacquiao gains weight and enters super featherweight division.
  • 2007 - Pacquiao officially announces he will run for a seat in the Philippine house of representatives. Pacquiao loses the election to Darlene Antonino-Custodio of the Nationalist People's Coalition
  • 2008 - Pacquiao fights Juan Manuel Marquez and wins by split decision to become WBC, The Ring and lineal superfeatherweight champion. He also becoems the first and only Asian boxer to become a four division world champion.
  • 2008 - Later on June 28 Pacquiao defeats David Diaz in the light weight division via a knockout in the 9th round to win the WBC Lightweight title. Pacquiao now becomes the only Asian to become a five division world champion and the first Filipino boxer to ever win a world title at light weight.
  • 2008 - On December 6th Pacquiao climbs up to the welterweight division to face six-division world champion Oscar De La Hoya for a non-title bout. Pacquiao is considered the underdog despite being recognized as the number one pound-for-pound boxer in the world due to fighting in a higher weight division. Pacquiao dominated De La Hoya until the 8th round where De La Hoya's corner threw in the towel.
  • 2009 - Pacquiao fights Ricky Hatton in the light welterweight division. Pacquiao knocks Hatton down twice in the first round. Then in the second round Pacquiao lands a left cross to the face of Hatton to knock him out -- winning the IBO, The Ring and lineal light welterweight title. He becoems the second man in boxing history to become a six division world champion.
  • 2009 - November 14, Pacquiao fights against Miguel Cotto for the WBO welterweight title. Pacquiao knocks Cotto down in round three and round four eventually leading to a victory by TKO in the 12th round. This win made Pacquiao the world's first seven division world champion in boxing history. Pacquiao is also awarded the WBC Diamond Championship belt, created in honor of a historic fight.
  • 2010 - Pacquiao is pitted up against Antonio Margarito for the vacant WBC Light Middleweight title. Pacquiao wins the fight via unanimous decision becoming boxing's only eight division world champion.
  • 2010 - Pacquiao runs for a congressional seat in Sarangani province under his own party the People's Champ Movement. Pacquiao gets elected over Roy Chiongbian and becomes a congressman.
  • 2013 - Pacquiao runs for his second term as congressman and is unopposed.
  • 2016 - Receives 16 million votes in the senate race in the Philippines, becomming the 7th out of 12 newly elected senators in the Philippines
